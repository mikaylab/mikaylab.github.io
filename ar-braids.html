bARaids | AR BRAIDS

Sci-Fi Prototyping Project

bARaids or AR Braids is an AR-enabled hair accessory that can be braided into someone’s hair and whose patterns can be read in an app to reveal the wearer’s hair story. The AR experience is meant to illustrate the cultural, social, emotional, and political significance that the wearer holds to the way that they wear, care, and relationship with their hair. The AR markers printed on the ribbon will be fractal patterns to reflect natural fractals in African braided hairstyles and compounding power of intergenerational storytelling.

This project was completed as part of the STAMPS Sci-Fi Prototyping course taught by Sophia Brueckner

Software & Tech

Adobe Illustrator, Apple Reality Composer, Maya, Spase.io, Adobe Dimension, Cricut

Process

I melded two ideas that I had for the semester long project that combined an interest on alternative means of communication and the cultural significance of braids.

I drafted 4 different AR markers to symbolize the different facets of braiding and Black culture: the tools, education, community, and aesthetics. Although these images were not distinct enough to be used as AR markers, they were a great prototypes to provoke thoughtful critique.

The 2nd iteration of the prototype included testing out braiding the printed ribbion into textured hair and rendering the 3D objects based on the AR symbols.
